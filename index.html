How it all started

  • 1969 the first two nodes that became ARPANET
  • Mainly used as tool to interact between research teams
  • 80's and the march of WWW
  • Modem and 56kbps

For reference, 361M is 2/3 of Facebook users today

Today - the problems

  • Internet as a whole has exploded
  • Logistical problems to accomodate every device
  • In our current protocol the IP-address space is limited to 2^32 addresses, about 4.2 billion
  • Every device that connects to internet needs one

Today - the fun stuff

  • Every piece of information is at your fingertips
  • And in many different formats
  • Communication is easy, a major milestone for globalization
  • Internet is equal
  • Internet is independent (no one has control over it)
  • You can effectively kill time

Future

  • Phasing to new IP, version 6. 2^128 addresses, 3.4 x 10^38. That is

340 000 000 000 000 000 000 000 000 000 000 000 000

  • That's 340 undecillion, or 340 billion billion billion billion (39 decimals). Is it enough?
  • Faster transfer speeds
  • Our lives will be even more tied to the internet, think augumented realities
  • We will leave a virtual footprint
